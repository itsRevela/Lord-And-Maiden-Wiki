## Damage formula & stat scaling   [damage-stat-scaling]
VALIDATION: medium confidence | {'confirmed': 8, 'partial': 8}

### SUMMARY
Combat is provably server-authoritative: the decompiled client (eb46ed1b3cbb.cs:38728) defines BehaviourRet as {TargetPos, RetType, RetVal, HPRate1, HPRate2} and HandleBehaviourRet (line 37489) just renders RetVal as the "Loss N" figure — no stats, coefficients, or DEF exist client-side, so the exact equation must be reverse-engineered from logged Loss values. Backing the formula out of the clean Baseline mirror (calibration_1) and the Pursuit log (calibration_3) shows direct damage is LINEAR in the per-hit skill coefficient (hit ≈ ~8,900 × coef, residuals <8%) but only WEAKLY scaling in hero ATK (loss ∝ ATK^~0.3, not ATK^1) and weakly in defender DEF (≈ DEF^-0.2 to -0.45). This is why the engine's offence floor (offence = soldier.atk 236 + hero.atk×0.20) reproduces the in-game per-instance ATK spread almost exactly (engine 1.16x vs real ~1.19-1.24x for a 521-vs-292 ATK gap) — the per-instance scaling is genuinely compressed and the engine is RIGHT here. The reported ~2x "+ATK out-damages +DEF/+DES" is NOT a per-instance scaling effect; it lives in (a) THROUGHPUT — +ATK pursuit carries fire far more skill instances (Niya 12 skills used / 65,355 kills vs +DEF Thiel 4 skills / 30,845 kills = 2.12x) — and (b) self-buff stacking (Mia's Divine Punish passive raises her in-battle ATK from ~521 to 989, log L278). The model's true gaps are: linear troop_scale (real attrition exponent ≈0.5, sub-linear), un-modelled in-battle ATK/DMG-Dealt self-buffs, and the over-counted multi-hit triggerChance that the DoT fit had absorbed.

### FORMULAS
  - Per damage instance (direct channels normal/tactical/pursuit), best fit to clean logs: Loss ≈ Kcaster × coef, where Kcaster ≈ 8,900 for a ~55k-troop Archer hero at ATK~500 vs DEF~190. Decomposing Kcaster: Kcaster ≈ G × ATK_eff^0.3 × Troops_attacker^0.5 × DEF_target^(-0.2..-0.45) × (soldier/army base). The coefficient multiplier is exactly linear; ATK, troops, and DEF enter as small powers.
  - ATK exponent (per-instance): p_ATK ≈ 0.27-0.35 (median ~0.3). Derived two independent ways: (i) baseline same-target Nicole ATK515→5149 vs ATK436→4927 ⇒ log(5149/4927)/log(515/436)=0.265; (ii) Mia in-battle ATK989→6457 vs Thiel ATK515→5149 ⇒ log(6457/5149)/log(989/515)=0.347.
  - DEF exponent (per-instance): q_DEF ≈ -0.2 to -0.46. From same-caster Chain Arrows: DEF192→6802 vs DEF403→4842 ⇒ log(4842/6802)/log(403/192)=-0.46; the 5-point baseline log-linear fit gave a weaker ~-0.2 (collinearity-limited). DEF mitigation is real but modest — a +229 DEF allocation (DEF 174→403) cuts incoming by only ~25-30%.
  - Attacker troop scaling: Loss ∝ Troops_attacker^~0.5 (SUB-linear). SusaMaki R1 5441@54k → R3 3363@21.5k ⇒ exponent 0.52. The engine uses exponent 1.0 (troop_scale = troops/55000), over-penalizing a wounded army.
  - Coefficient linearity holds for DoT too: tick ∝ coef (Burn coef1.0 = 2.23× Curse coef0.5 on same target), and tick ∝ caster troops (Burn 3982→674 as caster 45k→3k).
  - In-battle stat (combat_rules.json formula_shape, UNCONFIRMED magnitudes): in_battle_stat ≈ hero_stat×team_race_multiplier + troop_stat×(1+Σ gear/tech/title%). The static engine build (effective ATK 521) under-counts true in-battle ATK (Mia backs out to ~660 base before her +49.8% buff), implying additional multiplicative in-battle scaling not in the build step.
  - 'Affected by X / per-200' (community-stated, UNCONFIRMED): a stat-mod buff grants ~+1 unit per 200 points of the named caster stat. combat_rules.json attribute_scaling.coefficient_per_200 = UNKNOWN_SERVER_SIDE; no log read pins the exact per-200 value.

### ENGINE GAPS
  - model.offence (model.py:514) uses offence = soldier.atk(236) + hero_stat×0.20. The hero weight 0.20 is calibrated and the resulting per-instance ATK swing (1.16x for 521-vs-292) actually MATCHES the game (~1.19-1.24x). So this is NOT the bug — the engine correctly compresses per-instance ATK. The real fix is elsewhere (throughput + buffs).
  - model.troop_scale (model.py:521) = troops_now/55000 is LINEAR (exponent 1.0); the log implies exponent ~0.5. The engine over-penalizes attrition, making wounded armies (and the DoT caster, and late-round pursuit hits) hit too soft — a likely contributor to the DoT-vs-pursuit reconciliation failure.
  - Direct damage is provably LINEAR in the raw-token coefficient (hit ≈ 8900×coef), but the engine applies a level-scaling reinterpretation coef(L)=initVal+(maxV-initVal)(L-1)/9 that produced a WRONG Purgatory coefficient (1.95 instead of the token's 1.13), which is what created the false impression that coefficient scaling is 'compressed'. The engine should use the effect's raw-token coefficient directly.
  - In-battle self-buffs (at=9 ATK Attribute Increased, at=5 DMG Dealt, at=33 Witcher pursuit-dmg) are not folded into a unit's effective ATK during battle. Divine Punish nearly doubles Mia's ATK (521→989, log L278); omitting this makes +ATK carries under-deal and is a primary cause of the '+ATK heroes UNDER-deal ~2x' symptom (which is throughput+buff, not per-hit).
  - def_mitigation (model.py:526) = 600/(600+DEF×2) with hero_def_weight 2.0 makes DEF roughly twice as impactful as the logs show (real DEF exponent ~-0.2 to -0.46 means a 2.9x DEF gap cuts damage only ~25-30%). Combined with the missing throughput model, this over-rewards +DEF/+DES tanks — matching the reported '+DEF/+DES OVER-deal ~2x'.
  - Multi-hit triggerChance on at=101 (e.g. Rift 3@1.0 + 4@0.30 → ~4.2 hits): the FACT-correct fix cuts enemy AoE volume, but the DoT calibration absorbed the over-count as load-bearing enemy pressure. With troop_scale corrected to ~sqrt (gentler DoT decay) and direct coef fixed to raw tokens, the two should reconcile — the current linear troop_scale is the hidden coupling.
  - The static build (build_team) under-counts in-battle ATK even before skill buffs: Mia backs out to ~660 base in-battle ATK vs the engine's 521, suggesting a missing multiplicative in-battle stat step (combat_rules in_battle_stat.formula_shape) — race/tech/title multipliers applied at battle entry, not just additive flats.

## Multi-hit / AoE / proc semantics   [multihit-aoe-procs]
VALIDATION: high confidence | {'confirmed': 16, 'partial': 5}

### SUMMARY
Combat is server-authoritative: the decompiled client (eb46ed1b3cbb.decompiled.cs) only renders a resolved replay where each BehaviourRet = one already-computed hit on one target (TargetPos, RetType, RetVal=Loss, HPRate1/2). The client never parses the 12-token Effect/Buff/Dbuff strings for combat logic (it uses them only for description text and FX). The 12-token group format is therefore the SERVER's hit recipe, and it is fully readable in data/sim/skills.json + data/csv/NewSkillInfo.csv. A skill's hits are the union of all 12-token groups in its Effect, Buff and Dbuff columns: each actionType==101 group is one ATK WAVE that strikes token[3]=targetCount distinct targets, gated by token[4]=triggerChance, dealing token[5]=coefficient (the LEVEL-0 base; per-hit damage uses the leveled value initVal+Lv*upVal). targetCategory token[2] selects WHO: 2=Enemy Troops (SPREAD across N distinct enemies, one hit each), 0=inherit/focus the already-chosen target (re-hit the SAME unit). The logs validate this exactly (Rift = 3@1.0+4@0.30 -> 4 focused hits on Thiel; Chain Arrows = tcount 2 -> 2 distinct enemies; Binding Slash = 2 full waves x3 targets = 6 hits). Bonus pursuits use actionType 151/153 (server-side buff ids absent from Buff.csv) carrying their OWN coefficient and chance. The simulator's _fire_skill mis-models four things: it ignores per-effect triggerChance on 101 (over-counts), uses the skill maxedValue for every wave instead of the per-group leveled coefficient, ignores the spread(2)-vs-focus(0) target distinction, and silently drops all 151/153 bonus-pursuit hits. A same-caster/same-target log ratio analysis backs out a consistent normal_attack_coef ~1.05-1.10 and confirms per-hit damage is linear in the leveled coefficient.

### FORMULAS
  - Per-cast hit set = union over the skill's Effect, Buff, Dbuff columns of every actionType==101 group; for each group g: it produces g.targetCount hits IF rand() < g.triggerChance (each group is one all-or-nothing WAVE), each hit dealing the leveled coefficient. (Derived: skills.json effect_raw structure + log Rift/Binding Slash/Chain Arrows hit counts.)
  - Expected hits per cast = sum over 101 groups of (targetCount * triggerChance). Rift = 3*1.0 + 4*(1*0.30) = 4.2 (log observed 4). Sky Rain Arrows = 3*3 + 2*(3*0.40) = 11.4. Arrows Volley = 2*3 + 2*(3*0.40) = 8.4. (Derived from token chances.)
  - Leveled per-hit coefficient: coef(L) = InitValue + L*UpValue, with maxedValue = coef(10) = InitVal + 10*UpVal (FACT, decompiled:9942/9949). Skill stones are equipped at Lv5 -> coef(5) = InitVal + 5*UpVal (~25% below max). Token[5] stores the base InitVal.
  - Per-hit damage is linear in coef: dmg(hit) = coef(L) * (offence/troop/def factors that cancel for same caster+target). Cross-skill back-out gives normal_attack_coef ~= 1.05-1.10 (Rift lv5, Slayer lv10, Chain Reaction lv10 all converge), vs the simulator's assumed 0.9. (Derived from calibration_3 same-caster ratios.)
  - Targeting rule: targetCategory==2 (Enemy Troops) => pick targetCount DISTINCT enemies, spread one hit each; targetCategory==0 (inherit) => re-use the action's already-chosen target(s) => with count 1, all subsequent waves FOCUS that single unit. (Derived: Chain Arrows spread vs Rift focus in the log.)
  - Bonus-pursuit value = its OWN token coefficient, not the parent skill's: Flash Fire 2nd pursuit (151) coef 0.71; Trio's three 153 follow-ups coef 0.45 (parent main is 0.50). These hits exist only when their per-group triggerChance procs.
  - Trigger probability shown in log = base skillP + awaken(45_X) + rune/gear(131 tactical / 132 pursuit). e.g. Chain Reaction 40% + awaken8% + rune8% = '40.00%+16.00%'. (Derived: SkillAwake.csv + log suffixes.)

### ENGINE GAPS
  - combat.py _fire_skill (lines 496-513) IGNORES per-effect triggerChance on actionType==101: it fires every 101 group's full targetCount unconditionally. Rift fires all 7 waves (=>7-13 hits) instead of the expected ~4.2; Sky Rain/Arrows Volley/Binding Slash similarly over-count. This is the over-counting bug the findings flag (it was the load-bearing 'enemy pressure' the DoT calibration absorbed).
  - _fire_skill uses use_coef = skill maxedValue for EVERY 101 wave (lines 502-506), ignoring the per-group token coefficient. Wrong when follow-up groups carry a different coef (Trio main 0.50 vs 153 0.45) and wrong for Lv5 skill STONES (Niya's Rift stone should be coef(5)=0.375, not maxedValue 0.50 -- a 1.33x over-statement). Per-hit coef should be initVal + Lv*upVal.
  - Bonus pursuits 151/152/153/156 are SILENTLY DROPPED: the generator marks them isAction=False, so _fire_skill 'continue's past them (line 498-499) and _apply_skill_statuses (lines 525-582) has no 151/153 branch. Result: Flash Fire's 45% 2nd pursuit, Trio's three 153 follow-ups, and Chain Reaction's 151 deal ZERO damage in the sim (only the main 101 hit fires). grep of combat.py confirms no 151/153 handler exists.
  - _pick_attack_targets (lines 432-455) does NOT honor the spread(tcat=2) vs focus(tcat=0) distinction: for any non-commander/non-inherit category it always does weighted-squishiness sampling WITHOUT replacement, and for inherit it falls back to _pick_targets which UNIFORMLY re-samples per group rather than re-hitting the SAME focused target chosen by the first wave. The log proves inherit waves re-strike one fixed unit (all 4 Rift hits on Thiel).
  - The engine models a single coefficient per skill rather than a list of (coefficient, targetCount, targetCategory, triggerChance) WAVES. To match the client data it must iterate the actual 101 groups and roll each wave's chance, with focus-target memory for inherit groups.
  - Pursuit damage channel: model.py CHANNEL_PRIMARY_HERO_STAT maps pursuit->speed, but skills.json shows affectedByAttr==0 on every direct 101 effect (pursuit included) and ==1 only on Assault(70); calibration_3_findings #4 confirms pursuit DAMAGE scales with ATK, not Speed. The 'Affected By Spd' hint governs pursuit trigger/turn-order, not its damage coefficient.
  - The implied normal_attack_coef from the log (~1.05-1.10) differs from model.py's assumed 0.9; combined with the maxedValue-vs-leveled coef error this is part of why +ATK heroes under-deal ~2x and +DEF/+DES over-deal ~2x (offence floor compresses the spread while the over-counted waves inflate everyone equally).

The log shows the OPPOSITE structure: each pursuit (re)trigger is rendered as its OWN '[Hero] Use [Skill] Probability: X%' line followed by exactly ONE damage line. The Assault rider firing once per pursuit line (L300,L303,L360,L363,L366...) corroborates: each pursuit line = one attack event.

## Skill & stone LEVEL scaling + slot rules   [level-scaling-slots]
VALIDATION: high confidence | {'confirmed': 19, 'partial': 4}

### SUMMARY
The level->coefficient law is provable from the decompiled client and holds for all 416 skills: coef(L) = InitVal + L*UpVal for L in 1..10 (LINEAR, ADDITIVE per-level), rendered as a percent (x100%) when UpType is in {1,5-8,19-40,43-47}. This is NOT the (L-1)/9 interpolation hypothesized in the prompt, NOR the (maxedValue-initVal)*(L-1)/9 form written in notes/sim/calibration_3_findings.md line 25 -- those two laws agree only at L=10 and diverge at intermediate levels. Build levels are grounded in the in-game build sheets: main = lv10, modular = lv10 (max awakening), skill stones = lv5. Under the true client law a lv5 stone is exactly 75% of max coefficient (the findings' '~28% below max' / 72.2% figure is an artifact of its own wrong (L-1)/9 law). Skill LEVEL changes ONLY the coefficient -- the number of hits (separate at=101 effect groups), per-hit triggerChance, and targetCount are FIXED in data and do not scale with level; the sole exception is UpType==45 (27 skills) where the trigger PROBABILITY itself scales via the same init+L*up law. The skillStone flag reliably marks stones (it literally drives the client's 5-stone-prop generation loop). Slot rules: a stone is an optional 4th skill, 5 levels, with the only binding constraint being no two IDENTICAL stones per team (per-team, not per-battle); no per-category cap. ## Combat resolution mechanics (turn order, targeting, win condition) from the logs   [combat-resolution-logs]

### SUMMARY
Combat is fully server-authoritative: the decompiled client (decompiled/Assembly-CSharp/eb46ed1b3cbb.decompiled.cs) deserializes a precomputed RoundData list from the network where every FightBehaviour carries a fixed FightPos and its damage (BehaviourRet.RetVal) is read via proto.GetInt() (lines 19493-19531), never computed; there is no client-side speed sort or damage formula.   - No implementation of Tips Id=430 (commander death spawning many severe-wounded soldiers).   - Restraint (model.py restraint_mult) applies x0.75 to the restrained ATTACKER's outgoing damage — a reasonable but UNVERIFIED application point; untestable against the current logs (all Archer mirrors) so it is pure assumption with zero calibration coverage.

  - Stat ALLOCATION barely moves damage (calibration_3_findings.md: +ATK heroes under-deal ~2x, +DEF/+DES over-deal ~2x vs log) because offence() (model.py L514-518) is dominated by a soldier-ATK floor (236) with hero stat weighted only 0.20; this compresses the +229 allocation spread the in-game logs clearly show (~1.5-1.9x), and raising the weight breaks the baseline 50% mirror — the central sign the offence/mitigation structure is mis-specified.
  - The triggerChance-on-at=101 fix (correctly cutting over-counted multi-hit/AoE volume, e.g. Rift 4.2 not 7) is FACT-correct per skills.json but was reverted because it broke the DoT validator — proving the DoT fit had absorbed the over-counting bug as load-bearing enemy pressure; the engine cannot hold all 4 logs simultaneously, indicating missing real structure (most likely the skill-targeting/concentration rule plus the allocation->damage scaling), not just mistuned knobs.
