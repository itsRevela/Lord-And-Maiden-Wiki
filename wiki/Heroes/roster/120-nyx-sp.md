## Background
When hopeless redemption is repaid with gratitude, when the craving for life surpasses life’s own span; When desire swells beyond one’s own capacity, people begin to crave power no mortal should possess; O, ever‑insatiable ones, who still cling to a sense of entitlement until they meet the Azure Witch— Until the moment that azure flame ignites, all transgressive greed shall be burned to ash.

This was one among countless souls Nyx had aided, and she was growing weary. The human hunger for survival was terrifying in its intensity. A minor wound that would heal on its own, an illness soon to pass—yet magnified infinitely by fear. Upon merely hearing that Nyx could cure almost any ailment or injury, they charged forth like hyenas scenting prey. Fear of pain and sickness might be understandable, but to seek endless life—that was a transgressive delusion. People often say, “Without illness, there would be no death.” But is that truly so?

In her research, Nyx discovered that souls possess a certain energy, one that could burn for a very long time. And the souls of this world were nearly infinite, like the unending constant of pi. She burned the spirits of greed, turning them into inextinguishable flames; until greed was fully consumed, the fire would never die. Nyx burned away the excess greed in people’s hearts, while still upholding reverence for life. Yet those stripped of their surplus desires became content and at ease—which was not a good thing for the kingdom.

In the end, the kingdom launched a crusade against the Silent Altar. They could no longer bear a world so diminished of desire, for it had brought the kingdom’s progress to a standstill. Nyx was branded a witch. Whenever that wisp of azure soul‑fire appeared, desires were incinerated, diseases cured, and pain taken away. No matter what, let there be praise—chant the name of the Azure Witch: Nyx.
