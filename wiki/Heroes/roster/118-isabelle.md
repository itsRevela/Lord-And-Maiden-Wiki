## Background
In the early days of the Dawn Knight Order, surrounded by powerful enemies, every step was a struggle. Remnant forces of the old kingdom maliciously slandered them as blasphemous demons, and for a time, rumors spread that they committed every crime imaginable—burning, killing, and plundering. It was amidst this storm of public opinion that the young lady of the earl’s household, Isabelle, driven by a rebellious spirit born of privilege and a heart stirred by curiosity, saw that simple recruitment notice. Those around her warned, “That’s a den of demons, a knight order formed by devils—you mustn’t go!” But the words “mustn’t go” acted as the most effective catalyst. Isabelle lifted her chin defiantly. “I insist on seeing what this so-called den of demons really looks like.” And so, concealing her identity, she entered the gates of the Dawn Knight Order’s logistics department as an ordinary healing mage. This was the beginning of the story—though perhaps Isabelle would come to regret setting foot on this path of no return.

Upon joining the knight order, Isabelle observed everything with a critical eye. It was all so different from the noble life she knew: coarse food, humble barracks, faces weary yet resolute. She had expected to see chaos and brutality, but instead, she found a rough yet genuine order. Then she saw Aguria—that figure who shone like the sun on the battlefield. She watched Aguria return covered in dust and blood, tending not to her own wounds first, but comforting frightened civilians with a smile, even pulling a handful of candies from within her bloodstained breastplate to give to the children. In that moment, something in Isabelle’s proud heart trembled with an unfamiliar emotion. She murmured to herself, “Does she truly care so little for herself?” “I want a knight like that too—one who’s devoted entirely to me.”

During her days in the logistics department, Isabelle had more opportunities to observe Aguria. She saw how Aguria treated everyone—from fellow knights to wandering children—with the same innate, warm smile. That warmth fell equally upon all, like sunlight: generous, yet… cruelly distant. While inventorying battlefield relics, Isabelle came across a broken elven longsword. Though shattered, it still carried the violent energy imprinted by its former owner at the moment of death—an energy that clashed fiercely with the sword’s inherent light-aligned nature, creating a dangerous resonance within her. But she secretly kept it. Isabelle sought out Aguria, her gaze fervent, and said, “I don’t want to just cast healing spells from the rear anymore.” Aguria smiled and asked, “Then what would you like to do?” Isabelle instinctively tightened her grip on the broken sword’s hilt hidden in her sleeve, feeling a cold sting, and replied firmly, “I want to do more—to be closer to you.” Aguria didn’t catch the deeper meaning in her words. From then on, Isabelle used her family connections to secure more resources for the front lines, honed her magic more diligently, and began privately studying how to master that dangerous broken elven blade— all just to draw nearer to that radiant figure at the center of the light. The curiosity she had initially felt slowly fermented into a complex blend: recognition of Aguria’s convictions, admiration for her strength, and a flutter in her heart that she couldn’t quite name or dared not examine too closely—a desire to possess that warmth for herself. An unutterable, indescribable… love??? At the thought, Isabelle felt her heartbeat quicken, and the broken sword in her sleeve seemed to pulse faintly in response.

Isabelle attempted several times to hint at her special feelings. She would prepare finer healing salves for Aguria, saying, “This ointment works well—you should try it.” Beneath the bandages on her wrist were wounds carved by the sword’s violent energy. At victory banquets, she would pour wine only for her, softly toasting, “This cup is for you.” She would casually offer noble-style compliments laced with ambiguity: “You shine so brightly today.” Yet deep in her eyes flickered a trace of obsession amplified by the sword’s malevolent influence. But Aguria always responded with that trademark warm yet distant smile, thanking her for her kindness, easily dissolving her special attention into care for all her comrades, saying, “You’re so kind to everyone—I’m grateful.” Aguria was everyone’s little sun—not hers. This realization lodged like a tiny thorn in Isabelle’s heart. And the whispering broken sword in her sleeve continually tempted her toward more extreme thoughts. Watching the admirers surrounding Aguria, all basking in that same sunlight, Isabelle felt a stifling jealousy and frustration building inside her. The broken sword grew hot, as if responding to her emotions. The willful Isabelle had never felt so defeated—yet she was powerless for now. She couldn’t even bring herself to say “I like you” or “I love you” to Aguria.

The battle against Chidaru was exceptionally brutal. At the foot of the volcano, the sky was stained crimson by falling embers, and the earth trembled beneath the roars of monstrous creatures. As a battlefield healer, Isabelle never let her gaze stray from Aguria, who fought at the very forefront. In her right hand, she clutched the partially restored elven longsword—still seeping with ominous energy—its blade trembling faintly, thirsting for battle and destruction. She cast blessing spells, but the magic mingled with the violent energy radiating from the sword, twisting into warped ribbons of light that poured forth and coiled around Aguria. Layer upon layer of shifting light and shadow enveloped her knight—her Aguria. It was precisely because of this that Isabelle saw it all too clearly when a comrade suddenly thrust a poisoned blade toward Aguria’s back. Her terrified scream was swallowed by the chaos of battle. The venomous sword slid neatly through a gap in Aguria’s damaged armor. Aguria reacted with lightning speed, striking down the assailant in a single counterblow—but the poison had already seeped into her bloodstream amid the fierce combat. A wave of weakness washed over Aguria almost at once. Struggling, she propped herself up with her own sword. Watching Aguria stagger, Isabelle felt her own world crumble. The elven blade in her hand let out a sharp, piercing screech. “Aguria!!” Isabelle cried out in horror.

When Aguria finally collapsed from the poison and exhaustion, the taut string in Isabelle's heart snapped completely. Her long-suppressed emotions resonated with the violent energy stored within the elven sword, erupting like a volcano and drowning all reason. "No one touches my Aguria!"—this thought transformed into the most brutal form of magic. She no longer relied solely on spells—she swung the elven blade. The sword erupted in dark crimson light. Her once-gentle healing energy twisted and merged with the sword's malevolence, coalescing around her into countless magical blades woven of scarlet and shadow. Her killing intent became tangible, forming a terrifying force field that shredded any monster that drew near. She stood like vengeance returned from hell, treading upon swords forged from her own wrath, guarding the small sanctuary she had carved out with the last shred of her reason—a space only for her and Aguria.

The situation descended into an eerie stalemate. The arriving reinforcement knights found themselves in a treacherous position. On one hand, they had to defend against Chidaru and the monstrous horde; on the other, they had to remain wary of Isabelle—clearly deranged and hostile to all—and the ominous elven sword in her grasp. They glanced at their unconscious captain, then at Isabelle, who stood guard like a beast defending its prey, the malevolent energy of the blade pulsing around her. Someone murmured under their breath, “She and that sword… is she trying to save her, claim glory for herself, or… something worse?” Speculation and fear began to spread through their ranks like a shiver.

The Lord's arrival shattered the stalemate. Ignoring the threat posed by Chidaru, his gaze locked directly onto Isabelle and the ominously humming elven sword in her grasp. He advanced step by step, calling her name: "Isabelle! Calm down! Control the sword in your hand!"—attempting to pierce through her madness and reach her reason. His answer was a magical blade infused with dark energy that shot past his cheek, followed by an even denser wave of killing intent. Isabelle pointed her sword at the Lord, dark crimson energy continuously gathering around its form. When a second blade thrust straight for his chest, the Lord revealed his profound power, catching the nearly solidified blade of killing intent with his bare hand. The energy impact split his palm, sending blood streaming down. Yet his eyes remained unwavering as he pressed forward again, his voice steady and forceful, cutting through the darkness shrouding Isabelle's mind: "Isabelle! Look behind you! Aguria needs treatment!"

In Isabelle's distorted perception, the world was an endless darkness, with Aguria as the only source of light—and the sword in her hand was steadily expanding that darkness. All she wanted was to take this light and flee, to find a safe place. The Lord’s calls initially sounded like distant noise, until the name “Aguria” struck her ears again and again. She felt the elven sword in her hand grow scorching hot and heavy, as if resisting her return to clarity. It was as though a mist had been lifted—her vision gradually cleared. She saw the surrounding faces—some terrified, some worried, some angry. She saw the Lord’s bleeding hand, and she saw Aguria, lying pale and still within the protective glow. She looked down at the still-humming, sinister longsword in her hand, and a wave of belated fear washed over her. She murmured, “Aguria… I…” The weight of reality and the sword’s backlash instantly crushed her already exhausted mind and body. The elven sword fell from her grasp, its dark crimson glow fading, and her vision went black as she completely lost consciousness. The sky full of magical blades dissolved into scattered particles of light.

Isabelle spent long, torturous days in the confinement chamber. The troublesome elven sword had been confiscated, but its whispers seemed to still echo in her mind. The physical agony from the dual backlash of magic and the blade’s corrupting energy was nothing compared to the turmoil within. Over and over, she replayed every detail of that day: how the elven sword had amplified her emotions, how it had lured her to the brink of destruction. Her loss of control, the explosive killing intent, the moment she turned against her own comrades— and that near-annihilating obsession with Aguria buried deep in her heart… Had it already been twisted by the sword? She spoke to the wall as if to herself: “Was it love? A distorted desire to possess? Or merely the evil in human nature magnified by that blade?” She tried to define this burning, dangerous feeling with a clear label, only to find it was too complex to be captured by any single word. And the existence of that sword made everything even more elusive. All she knew was this: that emotion, combined with the sword’s power, was strong enough to destroy everything— even the one she most wanted to protect.

After Aguria regained consciousness and learned the full story—including the origin of the elven sword and its strange behavior during the battle—she personally released Isabelle from confinement. Facing Aguria, Isabelle was filled with guilt and unease. She hung her head and said, "I... I was wrong." "It was the sword... No, it was my failure to control myself..." But Aguria did not blame her. Instead, she sincerely thanked her for her desperate protection and even apologized for making her worry, saying, "Thank you, Isabelle. I'm sorry for making you fear for me." "Power itself holds no inherent good or evil—it all depends on the one who wields it." "It's alright, Isabelle. You did well." Aguria's gaze swept over Isabelle's empty waist, her glance carrying unspoken meaning. This life-and-death crisis unexpectedly drew the two closer. Aguria began seeking out Isabelle more often, sharing stories of her own experiences and cautiously discussing how to harness dangerous power. Their relationship grew into something closer than before—a bond akin to friendship.

Yet, for Isabelle, this newfound closeness was both torment and a warning. She clearly sensed that deep within Aguria, there remained a realm no one could ever touch—a space reserved only for past sorrows and a universal, impartial love for all living beings. And she herself could never forget the temptation and the feeling of losing control that the sword had brought. Isabelle learned restraint—not only of her emotions, but also of her thirst for power. She no longer ventured dangerous hints, instead burying those turbulent feelings deep within, transforming them into more precise protection on the battlefield and more efficient support in logistics. She asked Aguria to help her with control training, to learn how to face the darkness within—whether it stemmed from emotion or external forces. She needed that power to protect the one she loved. She still watched Aguria with focused intensity, but now her gaze held the clarity of vigilance and a wariness born from having tasted madness. Perhaps, for a sun destined to shine upon the whole world, being a star relatively close to her, sharing the same stretch of night sky, was already the greatest gift fate could offer. And that star must ensure it never burns too fiercely, lest it fall into darkness and scorch the very one it loves. Perhaps...
