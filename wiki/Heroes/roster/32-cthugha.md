## Background
Cthugha has a secret. By day, she is the revered saintess, but when night falls, she transforms into a judge, carrying out divine judgment against evil.

Unlike other saintesses, she never attends councils nor accepts unnecessary constraints. In Cthugha's world, good and evil are clearly defined, and if order cannot uphold justice, she will take it upon herself to do so. "I have no need to waste time with those long-winded troublemakers," Cthugha said.

There are many legends about her in the world, some deifying her, others slandering her. But regardless, with such a righteous enforcer around, most people feel at ease, and the number of monsters in the city has sharply decreased. Although the long stretch without wrongdoing feels a bit boring, Cthugha is quite pleased with this state of affairs.

For most of the day, she sat in the garden wearing an elegant long dress, sipping afternoon tea while listening to Bonnie's reports. She thought: "As expected of me, my methods are far more effective than Utija's."
