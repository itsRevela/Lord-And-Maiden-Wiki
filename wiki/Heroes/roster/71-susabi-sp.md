## Background
Susabi's departure did not quell the discussions among the beastmen; instead, it made her legend even more mysterious. The clan members began to link her appearances with significant events in the tribe, trying to find some pattern. However, Susabi's whereabouts remained as elusive as the wind. Each of her appearances seemed like a deliberate act of fate, yet they came without warning.

A few years later, the beastmen faced an unprecedented disaster—a strange plague spread through the tribe, causing the clan leader and many members to fall ill. As despair gripped the tribe, Susabi's figure once again appeared at the village entrance. She waved her nine tails, casting a gentle light. Those afflicted by the plague gradually recovered under this radiance, and Susabi's figure quietly vanished once more.

This time, the tribe's reverence for Susabi reached its peak. They began to build an altar in her honor, holding annual ceremonies to pray for her protection.

However, Susabi never appeared at the ceremonies. Her existence was more like a symbol, a force that transcended reality. And the secrets she sought might only be revealed by time.
