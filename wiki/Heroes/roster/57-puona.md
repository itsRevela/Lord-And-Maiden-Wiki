## Background
"So, am I ultimately just an unnecessary existence?" Puona didn't know where she was born. Her only childhood memory was that year in the woods, when Olrun smiled at her and said, "Don't be afraid, you have a home now."

Olrun had initially doted on her when she was young, but as Puona gradually matured, she discovered Olrun's secret. Like other adopted children, Puona was also required to train her slowly awakening spirit power. However, Puona seemed to lack talent, and consequently, Olrun grew increasingly cold toward her, even showing disdain.

She took on more household chores, dutifully cleaning, watering plants, and cooking every day without complaint. Yet these trivial tasks never earned her another glance from Olrun. In the end, she had lost that cherished affection. Olrun began assigning her improper tasks, but she could never bring herself to follow those orders, unable to go against her conscience. She knew Olrun would discover this one day.

Late at night, she sat on the rooftop, tormented by thoughts: "So, to Olrun, am I just an imperfect tool? Was everything in the past merely an illusion?"
